Soil surfaces with high soil crust factor tend to form a resistive layer to wind erosion as a protective mechanism (Fenta et al., 2020). This happens in places that are highly susceptible to wind erosion. The behavior of soil surfaces in these specific areas proves the Charles Darwin hypothesis of adaptation and survival for the fittest.
